Robot

The image below image shows my robot model rendered in Substance Painter after importing the final version.

The model was created for an assignment where we had to design a physical “puppet” capable of posing in a T-position that will then be imported into Mixamo for animation. My robot was first constructed physically using cardboard and hot glue — materials chosen for their accessibility and the raw, handmade aesthetic they bring to the piece. There’s a playful contrast in using such fragile materials to build a form typically associated with strength and technology.

Once assembled, I scanned the robot using LiDAR, imported the scan into Blender, and reduced the polygon count to make it suitable for animation. The final model was exported as an OBJ file.

Skull

The image below shows a VR sculpture created in Masterpiece Studio. It was exported as an FBX, then imported into Blender to be decimated and exported again as an OBJ file.

Surreal and strange themes - my own take on memento mori - have long fascinated me. These meditations on mortality, memory, and the absurd often find their way into my drawings, paintings, and sculptures. Working in VR has opened a new dimension in my practice, allowing me to bring these concepts into immersive, spatial forms. The immediacy and freedom of virtual sculpting let me explore ideas more intuitively, translating raw thought into shape without the limitations of physical materials. This piece reflects my ongoing interest in the intersection of life, death, and the dreamlike — a space where discomfort and curiosity coexist.
